Oolong tea loose leaf
£5.99
Description and how to brew

Oolong tea is a traditional Chinese tea that falls between black and green tea in terms of oxidation. It has a distinct flavor profile, ranging from floral and fruity to creamy and toasty, depending on the specific type of Oolong.

Nutritional Benefits

  • Rich in antioxidants that may help fight free radicals in the body.
  • May aid in weight management by boosting metabolism.
  • Contains polyphenols that could contribute to heart health.
  • May help improve mental alertness and focus due to moderate caffeine content.
  • Possesses potential anti-inflammatory properties.

How to Brew

Follow these steps to brew a perfect cup of Oolong tea:

  1. Use fresh, filtered water heated to around 185-205°F (85-96°C).
  2. Place 1-2 teaspoons of Oolong tea leaves in a teapot or infuser.
  3. Pour the hot water over the tea leaves and let it steep for 3-5 minutes.
  4. Adjust steeping time based on personal preference for strength.
  5. Strain the tea leaves and pour the brewed tea into your favorite cup.
  6. Enjoy the unique flavors and aromas of Oolong tea!

Indian chai tea loose leaf
£5.99
Description and how to brew

Indian Chai, also known as Masala Chai, is a traditional and popular tea in India. It is a spiced tea blend that typically includes a combination of black tea, spices, and herbs. The most common spices used in chai include cardamom, cinnamon, ginger, cloves, and black pepper. The blend is then brewed with milk and sweetened with sugar, creating a rich, flavorful, and aromatic beverage.

Nutritional Benefits

  • Rich in antioxidants that may help fight free radicals in the body.
  • May aid in weight management by boosting metabolism.
  • Contains polyphenols that could contribute to heart health.
  • May help improve mental alertness and focus due to moderate caffeine content.
  • Possesses potential anti-inflammatory properties.

How to Brew

Follow these steps to brew a perfect cup of Indian chai:

  1. Measure the desired amount of water for your tea and bring it to a boil in a saucepan.
  2. Add chai ingredients to the boiling water.
  3. Reduce the heat to simmer and let the tea steep for 2-5 minutes, depending on how strong you prefer your chai.
  4. Pour in the desired amount of milk. The ratio of water to milk can vary based on personal preference (some prefer equal parts water and milk).
  5. Sweeten (Optional) with sugar or honey

Matcha tea powder
£5.99
Description and how to brew

Matcha is a finely ground powder of specially grown and processed green tea leaves. It is known for its vibrant green color and unique flavor profile, which combines grassy notes with a hint of sweetness. Matcha is traditionally used in Japanese tea ceremonies and has gained popularity worldwide as a key ingredient in various culinary and beverage creations, such as lattes, desserts, and smoothies.

Nutritional Benefits

  • Rich in antioxidants that may help fight free radicals in the body.
  • May aid in weight management by boosting metabolism.
  • Contains polyphenols that could contribute to heart health.
  • May help improve mental alertness and focus due to moderate caffeine content.
  • Possesses potential anti-inflammatory properties.

How to Brew

Follow these steps to prepare a traditional Matcha tea:

  1. Sift 1-2 teaspoons of Matcha powder into a bowl.
  2. Heat water to approximately 175°F (80°C) and pour a small amount (about 2 ounces) over the Matcha powder.
  3. Whisk vigorously in a zigzag motion until the tea is frothy.
  4. Add more hot water to your bowl and whisk again, if desired.
  5. Enjoy the smooth and rich flavor of Matcha tea!
